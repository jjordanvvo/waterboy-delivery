Purity First

How We Purify Our Water

Every drop in our 5-gallon jugs passes through a rigorous multi-stage filtration process before it reaches your home or office. Here's exactly what that process looks like.

The Process

Multi-Stage Filtration

1

Sediment Pre-Filtration

Source water passes through a 5-micron sediment filter that captures visible particles — dirt, rust, sand, and other suspended solids. This protects the downstream filtration stages and extends their lifespan.

2

Activated Carbon Block Filter

A high-density carbon block filter removes chlorine, chloramines, volatile organic compounds (VOCs), and taste and odor compounds. This stage is what gives our water its clean, neutral flavor — no chemical aftertaste.

3

Reverse Osmosis (RO) Membrane

Water is pushed through a semi-permeable RO membrane at high pressure, removing up to 99% of dissolved solids, heavy metals (lead, arsenic), nitrates, fluoride, and other contaminants. This is the heart of the purification process.

4

Post-Carbon Polishing Filter

After RO, the water passes through a final carbon polish filter to remove any residual taste or odor from the storage and dispensing system. The result is water that tastes perfectly neutral and clean.

5

UV Sterilization

Before filling, all water passes under germicidal UV light that eliminates any remaining bacteria, viruses, and microorganisms — without any added chemicals or byproducts. Safe, pure, and ready to drink.

Alkaline Ionization (Alkaline Water Only)

For our alkaline water, a final ionization stage adds trace minerals (calcium, magnesium, potassium) and raises the pH to 8.5 or above. This is what gives alkaline water its smooth, slightly mineral taste and elevated pH.
