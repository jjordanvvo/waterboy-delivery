    How we filter

    Six Stages.
    Zero Compromise.

    01
    Pre-Filter
    Sediment Filter

    Water first passes through a 5-micron sediment filter that captures large particles — rust, dirt, sand, and debris. This protects downstream filters and extends the life of the entire system.

    02
    Carbon
    Activated Carbon Block

    A high-density carbon block adsorbs chlorine, chloramines, and volatile organic compounds (VOCs). This stage removes the chemical taste and odor common in municipal tap water.

    03
    Pre-Carbon
    Granular Activated Carbon

    A second carbon stage — granular activated carbon — provides additional chemical reduction before the water reaches the RO membrane, ensuring the membrane operates at maximum efficiency.

    04
    Core Stage
    Reverse Osmosis Membrane

    The heart of the system. Water is forced through a semi-permeable membrane with pores measuring 0.0001 microns — removing up to 99% of dissolved solids, heavy metals (lead, arsenic, chromium), fluoride, nitrates, and bacteria.

    05
    Sterilization
    UV Sterilization

    Purified water passes through a UV chamber that eliminates any remaining bacteria, viruses, and microorganisms that may have passed through previous stages. UV sterilization uses no chemicals.

    06
    Final Stage
    Post Carbon Polish & Remineralization

    A final carbon polishing stage removes any residual taste. For Alkaline water, trace minerals (calcium, magnesium, potassium) are then reintroduced at precise ratios to achieve pH 8.5+. For Hydrogen water, molecular hydrogen is infused at this stage.
