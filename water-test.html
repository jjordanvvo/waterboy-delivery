    What We Measure

    Four Key Water Quality Indicators

    Understanding these four measurements tells us almost everything we need to know about your tap water.

    Water Hardness

    Caused by dissolved calcium and magnesium. Hard water leaves scale on fixtures, reduces soap lather, and shortens appliance life.

    Measured in GPG or mg/L

    pH Level

    pH measures how acidic or alkaline your water is. Ideal drinking water sits between 6.5–8.5. Extremes can affect taste and pipe corrosion.

    Ideal range: 6.5 – 8.5

    TDS (Total Dissolved Solids)

    TDS measures all dissolved minerals, salts, and metals. High TDS can mean a flat taste, mineral buildup, or presence of unwanted contaminants.

    EPA secondary limit: 500 ppm
