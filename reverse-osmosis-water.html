What Is It

What Is Reverse Osmosis Water?

Reverse osmosis (RO) is a water purification process that uses a semi-permeable membrane to remove ions, molecules, and larger particles from drinking water. The result is exceptionally pure water with up to 99% of contaminants removed.

Unlike standard purification, RO filtration removes dissolved solids, heavy metals, fluoride, chlorine, nitrates, and many other impurities that typical filters leave behind. It's the gold standard in home water purification.

The Process

How Reverse Osmosis Works

Multi-stage filtration that removes what others can't.

1

Sediment Filter

Removes particles, rust, and sediment — the large stuff that clouds water.

2

Carbon Block

Removes chlorine, chloramines, and organic compounds that affect taste and smell.

3

RO Membrane

The core stage — a semi-permeable membrane that blocks dissolved solids, heavy metals, and contaminants at the molecular level.

4

Post Carbon Polish

Final polishing stage for any remaining trace elements, ensuring a crisp, clean taste every time.
